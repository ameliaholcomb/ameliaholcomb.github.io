About Me

I am a recent graduate from Yale University with a Bachelor's degree in Mathematics. In addition to my official major, I also completed all of the core courses required for the Computer Science major, as well as many of the electives.

My top interests in Computer Science lie in networking and algorithm design; I am particularly interested in the latter because of its close relation to mathematics. I also have industry experience in web development, including new libraries and frameworks such as AngularJS and React/Redux. In addition to writing code, I also have great familiarity with computer hardware, working to repair laptops as a Student Technician at Yale.

I am deeply committed to teaching Computer Science. While a student, I designed and taught the curriculum for training incoming Student Technicians, and I have also served as a Course Assistant and Peer Tutor for introductory and advanced computer science courses, respectively. This fall, I will be working on curriculum design, teaching, and course management with Yale's Computer Science Department.
